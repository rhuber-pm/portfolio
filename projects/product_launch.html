Project Overview

A comprehensive product development and launch initiative for Paragon Gear, focusing on bringing an innovative consumer product from concept to market. The project encompassed end-to-end management of design, manufacturing, supply chain optimization, and market launch strategy while coordinating with international manufacturing partners.

Our approach integrated robust quality control systems with agile manufacturing processes, establishing clear communication protocols with overseas partners through visual documentation and structured feedback loops. We implemented a hybrid project management methodology that balanced traditional stage-gate processes for development milestones with agile iterations for market testing and product refinements.

Through strategic planning and careful stakeholder management, we successfully navigated complex international manufacturing challenges while building a dedicated early adopter community. This systematic approach resulted in successful product launch, efficient supply chain operations, and the establishment of a scalable foundation for future product lines.

Hard-Won Lessons:
Product development hit a critical roadblock when our manufacturing partner revealed significant capability limitations that hadn't been disclosed during initial assessments. Instead of settling for lower quality or facing a 4-5 month delay, I quickly developed a two-track approach. We kept working with the original manufacturer on the parts they could handle well, while I found and onboarded a new partner for the critical components. The challenge was creating detailed specifications that could be handed off between manufacturers while protecting our intellectual property. By breaking down the production into modules and being clear about exactly what we needed from each partner, we managed to launch just 3 weeks behind schedule with better quality than we originally planned for. This taught me the importance of testing manufacturing capabilities early with actual production samples and creating thorough documentation that isn't tied to a single supplier.
